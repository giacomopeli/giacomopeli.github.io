A little hint about my Kobo#

I have owned a Kobo Clara HD for a few months now, and I must say that while I am an ardent supporter of reading in print, it has its charms. After using it quite a bit I can say that ereaders are extraordinarily useful and functional; everyone should own one. True, they are not multitasking devices, but that, in my opinion, is their forte: they do one thing and they do it damn well, without distraction.

Unfortunately, my Clara Hd came out in 2018 and the battery readings were completely starred. Despite showing the charge percentage at 40% the battery was completely drained. After trying them all I really think my Kobo does not provide a battery calibration mode, doing cycles from zero to one hundred percent was always useless. So I removed the battery from the Kobo and bought a circuit that charges lithium-ion cells. After soldering two terminals and printing a 3d case, I started charging the battery through this circuit board. It took me almost six hours. Now the battery seems to hold a charge and the Kobo seems to take true battery readings. The circuit is this, you can use it to recover many lithium batteries and it is quite inexpensive.

In writing this article, I was inspired by the one written by Anarcat on his blog. Also, if I ever reset my Kobo, I will just follow this article so that I will not forget anything. I hope it can be useful for you readers as well.

Let’s make it new#

We start from scratch: first we reset the Kobo, as if it were new.

We go to settings, then to device information and then to factory reset. From there, the device will begin restoring to factory settings.

In case you cannot get to this menu, you can do the reset by holding down the power button until we see the notice that the Kobo is resetting. This will not take long.

No account, thanks#

Once turned on, the Kobo will ask us to set the language, we select the language we want and reach the screen where we are asked to log in with our account.

Download sqlite3 to your computer, if you use Arch Linux type the command to open the file you want to edit via sqlite3

paru -S sqlite3

Connect the device to the computer, go to the .kobo folder and open a terminal. From there, type

sudo sqlite3 KoboReader.sqlite

When opening the cli interface of sqlite3 type the following command

INSERT INTO user(UserID,UserKey) VALUES('1','');

Since I will not be buying any ebooks from online stores I have no need to be logged in on my kobo, and besides, when I can protect my privacy it is good to do so.

Update to the latest firmware without logging in#

To install the latest firmware version you need to download the latest version for your ereader from here and extract the zip file in the .kobo directory. As you unmount your Kobo it will start to update, wait until it’s finished.

Transfer files to the Kobo with kobocloud#

I use syncthing on a daily basis to synchronize with a peer-to-peer network my files, and I could have used this very service to synchronize my books, but I happened to want to put a book in and not have access to my network or my main devices. I therefore discarded synchting as an option.

kobocloud allows me to keep a Google Drive folder synchronized on my Kobo. Every time the Kobo turns on or comes out of standby mode it checks to see if any files have been added to the Google Drive folder, in which case it syncs it and downloads the missing files. This way I can add ebooks to Google Drive from any device and from any account and be sure to find them on the Kobo. This service works very well and I highly recommend it.

To install it, download the latest KoboRoot.tgz from the release page of kobocloud. Put it in .kobo folder and disconnect your device from your computer. Wait until it’s updated.

Now give to your Google Drive folder the necessary privileges and copy the link into .add/kobocloud/kobocloudrc.

You can find it explained more detailed here.

More options with Nickel Menu#

By default, my Kobo Clara HD does not have necessary features enabled such as screen rotation, dark mode, and the ability to turn off and restart the device from the main menu. As in the previous steps, you download the KoboRoot.tgz file, copy it to the .kobo folder, disconnect the device, and wait until it has installed. Next, after reconnecting the Kobo to the computer, we go to the .adds/nm folder. Here we will find a file called doc, in this we find all the instructions and options that we can enable via nickel menu. It is important that we do not edit the doc file, but create one with any name, I called it nickelconfig. The settings I enabled can be found below.

#main_menu
menu_item:main:Portrait:nickel_orientation:portrait
menu_item:main:Landscape Right:nickel_orientation:landscape
menu_item:main:Landscape Left:nickel_orientation:inverted_landscape
menu_item:main:Shutdown:power:shutdown
menu_item:main:Reboot:power:reboot
menu_item:main:Dark mode:nickel_setting:toggle:dark_mode

#reader_menu
menu_item:reader:Portrait:nickel_orientation:portrait
menu_item:reader:Landscape Right:nickel_orientation:landscape
menu_item:reader:Landscape Left:nickel_orientation:inverted_landscape
menu_item:reader:Shutdown:power:shutdown
menu_item:reader:Reboot:power:reboot
menu_item:reader:Dark mode:nickel_setting:toggle:dark_mode

For more informations please read doc and navigate to this link.

Manage your library with Calibre#

Online ebooks can be found with different formats, but the best format for kobo is the .kepub format. Calibre allows us to use a folder on our computer as if it were a real library. We can put books in any format and then convert them, change covers, make collections, and arrange authors, series, publishers, and other things that will keep everything very neat. This folder is located on my server, so I can access this from any device on my local network, as long as it has calibre installed on the computer. The books I’m interested in I later drag them into the Google Drive folder, which will automatically sync.

Export notes and annotations#

When I read with printed paper I emphasize a lot, I could say, actually, that I struggle to read if in addition to the book I don’t also have a pencil in my hand. On the Kobo I can do this as well. For me exporting notes is very important, as I go through them on Overleaf and layout them with LaTex.

Exporting notes is a hidden feature and needs to be enabled.

Go to .kobo/Kobo/Kobo eReader.conf and add

[FeatureSettings]
ExportHighlights=true

Remove the Kobo’s connection with the computer and reboot. Holding down the book whose notes we want to export will bring up an Export highlights menu, clicking it will ask us what name we want to save the notes under, the format will be .txt. The notes will be saved locally on the kobo in the Exported Annotations folder.

Best fonts for an ereader#

On the various Kobo we can install the fonts we like best. This function, may seem trivial, but it is a useful function many people, especially for those who suffer from dyslexia.

To install fonts on a Kobo is very simple, just create a folder with the name fonts in the root directory and copy inside it the fonts we want to use for reading. Fonts can be installed in either .ttf or .otf format.

In my opinion you should try these fonts.

Fantasque Sans Mono

Roboto Condensed

Palatino

Atkinsons Hyperlegible

Reading and the five senses#

Ereaders are dumb and slow devices, but that is what makes them appeal to a wider and wider circle of people, and I think these can bring many people closer to reading. The eink technology can improve a lot and can have many applications in the future, just think of all the panels in a home automation home or the screens on household appliances.

I’m taking the Kobo on vacation this year. But what if it stops working? For that very reason I will also take paper books with me. Uereaders are not reading devices, but complementary to reading. One day a man who is passionate about music told me that man has five senses and that is why vinyl records still have value today. The same thing applies to books, the book you read tomorrow will not smell the same as yesterday’s book and the paper will probably be rougher and perhaps heavier. Printed paper manages to find its own identity to this day. An electronic device can hold an entire library inside, but it can never replace the reading experience of holding a paper book in your hands.
